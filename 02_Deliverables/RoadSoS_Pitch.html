2Real-Life Scenario — A Day with Ravi

Ravi commutes from Hosur to Bangalore on NH-44. Here's how his RoadSoS helmet works across an ordinary, rainy day.

06:30Boot & charge. Helmet powers on. Green LED pulses — battery 92%, solar trickle active. Medical profile (O+, asthma, contact: wife Anjali) encrypted on ESP32-S3.

07:12Rain + oil patch at km 47. IMU detects abnormal lateral acceleration and a 23° lean angle building too fast. On-device PINN estimates skid probability past 0.65. Buzzer pulses, brow LEDs flash amber, haptic motor taps Ravi's temple. He eases the throttle. Crisis averted.

11:40Truck swerves; Ravi is thrown. IMU registers > 6 g impact + free-fall + sudden tilt > 80°. Edge-AI 1D-CNN confirms crash (not pothole, not dropped helmet). 10-second cancel countdown begins.

11:40:10Emergency mode. GPS locked. SIM7600 dispatches SMS + HTTPS to 108, hospital API, police, and Anjali. LoRa 433 MHz broadcast — every RoadSoS helmet within ~3 km buzzes. Beacon mode: rear LEDs strobe red, buzzer chirps Morse SOS. A bystander scans the QR on the shell — first-aid + blood group instantly visible.

11:54Ambulance arrives — Golden Hour preserved. Three riders 1.5 km behind have already slowed because their helmets vibrated. No secondary collision.

16:00Return ride. Daytime sun has been trickle-charging the helmet — battery is at 87% despite 8 hours of operation.

5Edge AI — Two Models, One Chip

RoadSoS runs two complementary models on the same ESP32-S3, one per FreeRTOS core. The PINN handles the before (prevention). The TinyML classifier handles the during (confirmation). Together they cover the full safety timeline — neither alone would.

🧠 PINN — Physics-Informed NN

What: predicts rider instability before loss of control. Outputs P(skid), P(tipover), time-to-critical each 100 ms.

Why physics-informed: a vanilla NN needs millions of labeled crashes. PINN bakes the motorcycle dynamic equations into the loss function — generalises from small data.

Loss = MSE + λ · ‖ I·φ̈ − m·g·h·sin(φ) + m·v²·h·cos(φ)·δ/L ‖²
+ μ · ‖ √(a_x² + a_y²) − μ·g ‖₊ (friction circle)

Where it runs: ESP32-S3 core 1 · MLP 4 × 32 · 12 KB INT8 · ~3 ms latency.

⚡ TinyML — 1D-CNN Crash Classifier

What: confirms whether an impact is a real crash (vs. dropped helmet, pothole, speed bump, hand slap).

Architecture: 1D-CNN, 3 conv + 2 dense, INT8-quantized. Input: 200 ms sliding window of 6-axis IMU (50 Hz × 200 ms = 60 × 6).

Training data: SisFall + UMA Fall public datasets + 1,200 self-collected motorcycle runs across rough Indian roads.

Where it runs: ESP32-S3 core 0 with ESP-NN acceleration · TensorFlow Lite Micro · 48 KB · ~9 ms latency.

6Emergency Response Flow

From impact to ambulance dispatch in under 15 seconds. Every millisecond accounted for.

1
10-second cancel window

Rider can press the chin-strap button to abort false alarms — protects against a helmet that fell off a parked bike.

2
Four parallel channels

Cellular (108 + hospital + contacts), LoRa mesh broadcast, BLE phone redundancy, and on-helmet beacon all fire simultaneously.

3
Medical profile attached

SOS packet carries blood group, allergies, conditions, and emergency contacts. Bystanders scan the QR for first-aid.

4
Forensic log

Telemetry from 30 s before impact stored on PSRAM — useful for insurance and law-enforcement playback.

7LoRa Rider Mesh

When one helmet crashes, every helmet within ~3 km knows in under 2 seconds. No cell tower required — works in dead zones and rural areas.

📡 433 MHz, WPC-licensed

Free band in India. Sub-mW idle. 120 mA TX burst — barely registers on the battery budget.

🔁 TTL-2 relay

Each helmet re-broadcasts once, doubling effective range without flooding the network.

🛡 Stops secondary collisions

5 seconds of early warning is enough to brake from 80 km/h to a survivable speed.

8Solar Charging — Power from the Sun

Flexible amorphous-silicon solar film on the curved top shell trickle-charges the helmet during daylight rides. On a typical 6-hour daytime commute, the helmet gains charge instead of losing it.

☀ a-Si flexible film

Conforms to the helmet's curve. Shatter-resistant — no glass anywhere near the rider's head.

⚡ MPPT (CN3791)

Extracts maximum power even under partial shade (visor, head tilt). Linear TP4056-class charger with OV / OC / OT cutoff.

🔋 Adaptive duty-cycle

Lux sensor switches the helmet into power-saving mode at night (GPS duty-cycled) and full-active mode in daylight.
